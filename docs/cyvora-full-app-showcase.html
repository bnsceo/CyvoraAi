Safety and control

Autonomy without losing control.

The public page explains the operating model without exposing private access codes, worker endpoints, database credentials, or runtime control paths.

What stays public

✓
Product narrativeThe operating model, hierarchy, and founder workflow are visible.
✓
Static visualsMocked screenshots and shell previews communicate the experience safely.
✓
Public navigationCommand Center, HQ, Companies, and War Room are explainable from the outside.

What stays private

✓
Access codes and authUnlock flow stays inside the live application, not on Pages.
✓
Workers and endpointsBackground execution and private APIs are excluded from the static site.
✓
Credentials and databasesNo secrets, tokens, or real connectors are exposed in the showcase.

Mobile-first shell rules

The local app stays desktop-clean and mobile-complete.

The sidebar is hidden by default, opens through the hamburger, and the bottom dock only appears on phones. On desktop, the dock stays out of the way.

Desktop

One hamburger. No dock. No close X.

Desktop view keeps the workspace uncluttered. The top-left hamburger toggles the sidenav, and the pages remain visible behind it instead of frosting out.

Mobile

Dock flush to the bottom edge

The mobile dock is anchored to the bottom, with no desktop presence and no extra layout gap.
